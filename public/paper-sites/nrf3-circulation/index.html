Nrf3-Mediated Mitochondrial Superoxide Promotes Cardiomyocyte Apoptosis and Impairs Cardiac Functions by Suppressing Pitx2

Qishan Chen, MD, PhD; Ancheng Zheng, MSc; Xiaolei Xu, MD; Zhenning Shi, MD; Mei Yang, PhD; Shasha Sun, PhD; Leyu Wang, MB; Yumeng Wang, PhD; Haige Zhao, MD; Qingzhong Xiao, MD, PhD; Li Zhang, MD, PhD

Department of Cardiology, Xinhua Hospital, Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine

Myocardial Infarction Mitochondrial ROS Cardiomyocyte Apoptosis Nrf3 Pitx2 DNA Methylation

Abstract

Background: Myocardial infarction (MI) triggers mitochondrial reactive oxygen species (ROS) production and cardiomyocyte (CM) apoptosis. Nrf3 (nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 3) has a well-established role in regulating redox signaling and tissue homeostasis. This study aimed to evaluate the role and mechanisms of Nrf3 in injury-induced pathological cardiac remodeling.

Methods: Global (Nrf3-KO) and cardiomyocyte-specific (Nrf3ΔCM) Nrf3 knockout mice were subjected to MI or ischemia/reperfusion injury, followed by functional and histopathological analyses. Primary neonatal mouse and rat ventricular myocytes and human iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes were used to evaluate the effect of Nrf3 on CM apoptosis and mitochondrial ROS production. ChIP-seq and IP-mass spectrometry were performed to discover potential targets of Nrf3.

Results: Nrf3 was primarily expressed in cardiomyocytes of healthy human hearts, and elevated Nrf3 levels were observed in border zone cardiomyocytes of infarcted human hearts and post-MI mouse cardiac tissue. Both global and cardiomyocyte-specific Nrf3 knockout significantly reduced injury-induced mitochondrial ROS production, CM apoptosis, and pathological cardiac remodeling, thereby improving cardiac function. Mechanistically, Nrf3 was found to bind the Pitx2 promoter and recruit the hnRNPK–DNMT1 complex to increase DNA methylation, thereby suppressing Pitx2 expression. Cardiomyocyte-specific knockdown of Pitx2 abrogated the beneficial effects of Nrf3 deletion, while cardiac-specific overexpression of Pitx2 attenuated MI-induced mitochondrial ROS production and CM apoptosis and preserved cardiac function after MI.

Conclusions: Nrf3 promotes injury-induced CM apoptosis and deteriorates cardiac function by increasing mitochondrial ROS production through suppression of Pitx2 expression. Targeting the Nrf3–Pitx2–mitochondrial ROS signaling axis may represent a novel therapeutic approach for MI.

Key Findings

  • Nrf3 is elevated in border zone cardiomyocytes of infarcted human and mouse hearts, implicating its role in cardiac injury response.
  • Global and cardiomyocyte-specific Nrf3 knockout protect against MI-induced mitochondrial ROS, apoptosis, and pathological remodeling.
  • Nrf3 promotes CM apoptosis across three species: mouse, rat, and human iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes.
  • Nrf3 binds the Pitx2 promoter and recruits the hnRNPK–DNMT1 complex to silence Pitx2 expression via DNA methylation.
  • Cardiomyocyte-specific Pitx2 overexpression attenuates MI-induced mitochondrial ROS and preserves cardiac function post-MI.
  • The Nrf3–Pitx2–mitochondrial ROS axis is identified as a novel therapeutic target for myocardial infarction.
