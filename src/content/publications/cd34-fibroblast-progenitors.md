This study published in *Circulation Research* reveals that circulating CD34+ fibroblast progenitors play a critical role in cardiac fibrosis of allografts following heart transplantation. The key findings include:

- Circulating CD34+ cells with fibroblast differentiation capacity are recruited to the allograft heart and contribute to interstitial fibrosis.
- These progenitors originate from the bone marrow and are mobilized in response to transplant-associated injury signals.
- Targeting the recruitment or differentiation of CD34+ fibroblast progenitors may represent a novel therapeutic strategy for preventing cardiac allograft fibrosis.
