Published in *Cardiovascular Research*. This study examines the role of the Nrf3-Trim5 axis in vascular smooth muscle cell dysfunction and neointimal hyperplasia.

Nrf3 regulates smooth muscle cell phenotypic switching and proliferation through transcriptional control of Trim5. In vascular injury models, targeting the Nrf3-Trim5 pathway attenuates neointimal hyperplasia. These findings suggest a mechanism for modulating vascular remodeling in proliferative vascular disease.
