This study published in *Cardiovascular Research* reveals novel roles of the Nrf3-Trim5 axis in vascular smooth muscle cell (VSMC) dysfunctions and neointimal hyperplasia. The key findings include:

- Nrf3 regulates VSMC phenotypic switching and proliferation through transcriptional control of Trim5.
- The Nrf3-Trim5 signaling axis mediates VSMC dysfunction in response to vascular injury.
- Targeting the Nrf3-Trim5 pathway attenuates neointimal hyperplasia in animal models.
- These findings identify a potential therapeutic target for vascular proliferative diseases such as restenosis and atherosclerosis.
