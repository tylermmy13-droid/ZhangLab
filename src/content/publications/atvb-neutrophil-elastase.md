This study published in *Arteriosclerosis, Thrombosis, and Vascular Biology* (Volume 43, Number 10) reveals a causal role for neutrophil elastase in thoracic aortic dissection. The key findings include:

- NE expression and plasma activity are significantly increased in BAPN-induced TAD mouse models and acute TAD patients.
- NE gene deficiency prevents BAPN-induced TAD development, while pharmacological inhibition with GW311616A ameliorates TAD formation/progression.
- Reduced levels of NETs, inflammatory cells, and MMP-2/9 are observed in NE-deficient mice.
- TBL1x is identified as a novel substrate and functional downstream target of NE in TAD.
- NE regulates inflammatory cell transendothelial migration via the TBL1x–LTA4H signaling pathway.
- NE modulates smooth muscle cell phenotype through the TBL1x–MECP2 signaling axis under TAD pathological conditions.
- The NE–TBL1x signaling axis represents a potential therapeutic target for patients at high risk of TAD.
