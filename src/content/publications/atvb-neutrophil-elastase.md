Published in *Arteriosclerosis, Thrombosis, and Vascular Biology* (Volume 43, Number 10). This study establishes a causal role for neutrophil elastase in thoracic aortic dissection using mouse models and human tissue analysis.

Neutrophil elastase expression and plasma activity are elevated in both BAPN-induced mouse models and acute human dissection samples. Genetic deficiency or pharmacological inhibition of elastase with GW311616A reduces lesion formation, decreases NET formation, and attenuates inflammatory cell infiltration. TBL1x is identified as a downstream substrate; the elastase–TBL1x axis modulates transendothelial migration via LTA4H and smooth muscle phenotype through MECP2.
