This study published in *Circulation* examines the relationship between subendocardial perfusion and myocardial injury, cardiac structure, and clinical outcomes in patients with hypertension. The key findings include:

- Impaired subendocardial perfusion is independently associated with higher levels of myocardial injury biomarkers.
- Reduced coronary flow reserve correlates with adverse cardiac remodeling and increased left ventricular mass.
- Subendocardial perfusion defects are linked to worse clinical outcomes, including increased risk of heart failure hospitalization.
- Quantitative PET perfusion imaging provides a noninvasive approach to assess the subclinical cardiac effects of hypertensive microvascular dysfunction.
